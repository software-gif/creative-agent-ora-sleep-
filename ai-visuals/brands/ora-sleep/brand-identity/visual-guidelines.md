# Ora Sleep — Visual Guidelines

## Brand identity
**Name:** Ora Sleep (logotype stylised as lowercase `ōra` with macron over the "o")
**Tagline:** Schlafkomfort aus der Schweiz
**Design agency:** In-house / unattributed
**Positioning:** Swiss-made, direct-to-consumer premium mattresses researched and built in a Zurich sleep lab, sold to German-speaking DACH customers who want "Testsieger"-quality without retail markup.
**Voice:** Swiss-grounded, data-led, du-form-direct, calmly confident, unpretentious (five precise adjectives — not "clean" or "premium")
**What sets it apart:**
- vs. **Simba Sleep (UK)**: Ora leans hard on *local* Swiss manufacturing and Kassensturz/Testsieger credibility; Simba leans on UK design pedigree and heavier paid-media production values.
- vs. **Emma**: Ora is deliberately less slick and more founder-direct; Emma's creative is polished corporate — Ora trades that for Andromeda-style creative diversity.
- vs. **Avocado (USA)**: Ora's story is Swiss precision and sleep-lab data; Avocado's is organic materials and sustainability — Ora doesn't compete on eco narrative.

## Colour palette
- **Primary:** Charcoal — `#272727`
- **Secondary:** Navy — `#3C619E`
- **Accent:** Success green — `#708A5C`
- **Background:** Pure white `#FFFFFF` / light grey `#F2F2F2` (site uses both; white dominates, grey for section breaks)
- **CTA / button:** Fill `#272727` with white `#FFFFFF` text, radius `0.375rem`, medium weight, no shadow — hover inverts to outlined. Secondary CTA fill `#3C619E`.

> **Diversity note (from kick-off):** These are the *website* colours, not creative constraints. The top-performing Ora ad ("93 Prozent") used plain high-contrast black-on-white text, not the brand palette. Creatives may use bold neons, pastels, gradients, warm earth tones — whatever performs.

## Typography
- **Headline:** Jost — weights 400–700, headline letter-spacing `-0.01em` (tight). H1 scale 2.5rem–3.75rem.
- **Body / UI:** Jost — weight 400, neutral tracking.
- **Treatment:** Tight tracking on display sizes, generous leading on body, sentence case (no all-caps as a system rule), confident but never loud.

> **Diversity note:** Jost is the website font. Creatives have ~80 fonts in active rotation — serif, display, handwritten, meme-style — whatever fits the angle. No font is mandatory.

## Photography style
- **Lighting:** Soft, directional, single-source studio light — gentle shadow falloff, natural feel despite being set-built. No hard flash, no flat cyc-wall.
- **Colour grade:** Warm, monochromatic *set design* grading — hero imagery is shot on tonal colour-blocked backdrops (dusty terracotta, clay rose, soft sand, muted warm greys). Skin tones preserved neutral; surfaces push warm. Low contrast, medium saturation, matte — not glossy catalogue.
- **Composition:** Centered, product as architectural anchor (the bed frame as black linear structure against coloured wall), models in relaxed off-duty poses *on top of* the mattress rather than tucked in. Generous negative space, deliberate props (book, mug, sculptural lamp, ceramic vase).
- **What appears in frame:** Couples and solo sleepers in loungewear (pyjamas, soft knits), reading, drinking coffee, laughing — calm morning moments, never sleeping. No children in hero shots observed. Minimal smiling-at-camera; naturalistic candid energy.
- **Surfaces and backgrounds:** Matte-painted set walls in single warm tones, matching floor, minimal art-direction props (curved pastel sculptures, paper screens, simple floor lamps). No real bedrooms — the aesthetic is editorial set design, not home photography.
- **Overall mood:** calm, grounded, unhurried, considered, tactile (5 adjectives)

> **Diversity note:** Lifestyle creatives may break from the tonal-set look entirely — UGC iPhone selfies, real bedrooms, data-card statics, testimonial frames all belong in the rotation.

## Packaging & product
- **Physical form:** Standard rectangular mattress (not rolled in hero shots — presented flat on a minimal black steel bed frame). Light grey cover fabric, white top surface, visible handles on side panel.
- **Label and logo placement:** Small `ōra` wordmark embroidered bottom-right of the side panel — stealth, not a billboard hit. No front-facing brand badge.
- **Distinctive visual features:** Black linear steel bed frame acts as a graphic frame around the mattress in all hero shots — it's as much a brand signature as the wordmark. Two-tone cover (white top, grey side). Clean geometric silhouette.
- **Product system:** Only two products (Ora Ultra Matratze + Ora Ultra Topper). Both share the same grey/white fabric language and the same "Swiss Made" story. The range is unified through restraint, not through SKU proliferation.

## Ad formats & creative style
- **Formats:** 4:5 (Feed), 9:16 (Story/Reel), 1:1 (Square). All three required. Currently **statics only** — video is a follow-up project.
- **Text on image:** Highly variable — the top-performing ad is plain high-contrast type over a flat background ("93 Prozent"). Other creatives use data points, customer quotes, bold statements. No fixed type treatment or lockup.
- **Photo vs illustration:** Mostly photography (product stills, tonal lifestyle, UGC). Illustration appears occasionally in data-card formats.
- **UGC:** Yes — creator-to-camera content is in rotation, often Swiss-German voiceover. Treated plain, not colour-graded to match the warm set aesthetic.
- **Pricing and offers:** Price is shown when it's the lede (Testsieger value angle); otherwise creative leads with angle first. Discounts are presented as CHF values, not % off, paired with the old-price strikethrough.

> **HARD GUARDRAIL — Health Claims:** Never promise cures or medical outcomes ("heilt Rückenschmerzen", "garantiert besseren Schlaf", "klinisch getestet"). Always phrase as *customer-reported* ("49% berichten von Schmerzlinderung", "60% schlafen in unter 10 Minuten ein"). Violation risks Meta ad-account blacklist — the client's single largest concern.

## Prompt modifier
Swiss editorial mattress photography: tonal warm set design in dusty terracotta, clay rose or soft sand (`#272727` charcoal accents, never pure black), single soft directional studio light with gentle shadow falloff, low-contrast matte colour grade — never glossy. Product on a slim black steel bed frame against a matching warm-tonal painted backdrop, minimal sculptural props (curved lamp, ceramic vase, book). Models in loungewear, candid unhurried morning moments. Calm, grounded, unpretentious — Swiss restraint, not luxury gloss.
